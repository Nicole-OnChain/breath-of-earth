## WHAT THIS EPISODE DOES

Lyra comes back. She uses her wind to create a distraction — not a weapon, a shield. While the enforcers respond to the windstorm, she finds Ronan in the holding cell.

The bars are ancient — old stone and iron that an Earth-shaper alone cannot break. But together, her wind and his earth push them apart. The first time two elements work together. The first proof that the hierarchy is wrong about them.

He steps through. They reach for each other. Almost kiss. Almost. Then he holds out his hand, she takes it, and they run.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES

### Lyra
She came back. Ep 8 — her wind exploded. Ep 9 — he saved her. Ep 10 — she found him, his hand moved toward hers. Ep 11 — she's getting him out. She uses her wind as a distraction, not a weapon. She's learned control. And when the ancient bars won't yield to him alone, she pushes with her wind while he pushes with his earth. Together.

### Ronan
He's been in that cell since Ep 9. Punishment marks. He didn't expect anyone to come. When she reaches through the bars, his hand meets hers — all the way. And when she tells him to push, he pushes. The earth responds. But the bars are ancient — they don't yield to one element. They yield to two. When he steps through, he reaches for her. Almost. Not quite.

### The Bars
Ancient. Old stone and old iron. Built before the hierarchy, maybe. Something that one element alone cannot break. But wind pushes while earth pushes, and the bars give way. The first proof of what the hierarchy fears most.
