## Story

In a world where people are born with elemental powers and ranked by them, a Wind-weaver discovers her gift is far more dangerous than anyone knows - and that the quiet Earth-shaper who saved her may be the key to breaking a system designed to keep them apart.

## Characters

### Lyra (Wind-weaver / POV Character)
- New to her powers - erupted at the party (Ep 1) when cornered
- Grew up in a happy, normal family. Two sets of twins. No one knew about elements.
- Twin sister Sera (no element) and two red-headed little brothers (Fire, later)
- Loving mom and dad - happy childhood in the middle of nowhere
- Lyra's core fear: she got pulled into this world and her whole family got dragged in with her
- Doesn't accept the hierarchy, instinctively rebels
- **Voiceover:** Venice Studio TTS (Orpheus or ElevenLabs Turbo) OR Nicole's own voice recorded and uploaded
- **Visual:** Young woman with windswept silver-blonde hair, fair skin, wide expressive eyes, lean build, white/silver flowing fabrics

### Sera (No Element / Future Villain)
- Lyra's twin sister - they look alike, but "that's where it ends"
- **No element** - in a world defined by elements, she's nothing
- Grew up in the same happy, normal family. Didn't know elements existed until Lyra's erupted.
- Later: learns to **steal energy** from people and the land (bad magic, taught by a mysterious man)
- Becomes the villain in a later season
- In Season 1: just a shadow, a seed, something off - calculating, watching, not grateful
- Her isolation cuts deeper because *everyone* in her family has something. Lyra has Wind. The boys will have Fire. Mom and Dad loved them all the same - but the world doesn't.
- **Visual:** Almost identical to Lyra but with darker, harder eyes and brown-blonde hair instead of silver-blonde. Where Lyra looks lost, Sera looks calculating.

### The Twins (Red-Headed Little Brothers / Future Fire)
- Two identical red-headed boys, around 8 years old, freckled and wild
- They haven't manifested yet - will become Fire element in a later season
- In Season 1: background characters, a flash of red hair, sleeping beside Lyra
- They deepen Sera's isolation - everyone in this family has something coming. Everyone except her.
- Don't name them yet. Don't explain them. Just show they exist.
- **Visual:** Two identical red-headed boys, freckled, mischievous, simple worn clothing

### Mom and Dad (Flashback Only)
- Loving, warm, normal - raised four kids in the middle of nowhere
- Didn't know about elements either. None of the kids did. Happy childhood.
- Appear only in flashbacks - Lyra's memory of who she's fighting to get back to
- **Visual (flashback):** Warm lighting, simple home, mom with silver-blonde hair like Lyra, dad with dark hair and a kind smile, all four kids together, golden hour, safe
- Don't over-explain them. Just warmth. Just home. Just what Lyra lost.

### Ronan (Earth-shaper)
- Silent, powerful, forbidden
- Doesn't speak much - his actions speak (earth moves with him, ground shifts for him)
- Takes the fall for Lyra in Ep 9 - uses earth magic to protect her from enforcers, gets punished for it
- The audience will be obsessed with him by Ep 2
- **Visual:** Tall, muscular, dark hair, stoic expression, earth-toned clothing (browns, deep greens, black leather), imposing presence, backlit

### Zara (Flame-kissed)
- Red-haired, confident, prissy-sarcastic, warm with an edge
- Calls Lyra "sweetheart" and "princess" - means them warmly, also marking territory
- Benefits from the system but genuinely believes it's how the world works
- History with Caspian (complicated, unresolved - the look says it all)
- Gives hierarchy tours like real estate listings: "Better air flow. Obviously."
- The kindness and the cage are the same gesture - that's what makes her dangerous
- **Visual:** Red-haired woman, confident smirk, flame-red and gold clothing, amber eyes
- **Note:** Zara is NOT related to Lyra, Sera, or the brothers. Different family. Her red hair is coincidental - it's a Fire-element trait, not a family trait.

### Caspian (Tide-caller)
- Striking dark-skinned man, light compelling eyes
- Blunt, perceptive, challenges Lyra
- Has history with Zara - electricity despite hostility
- **Visual:** Dark-skinned man with striking light-colored eyes, blue-teal clothing, water mist around him, calculating expression

## World Rules

| Element | Class | Position | Powers |
|---------|-------|----------|--------|
| Wind | Weaver | 1st (top) | Wind control, storms, air manipulation |
| Fire | Flame-kissed | 2nd | Fire creation, heat, passion-fueled |
| Water | Tide-caller | 3rd | Water control, mist, tidal forces |
| Earth | Shaper | 4th (bottom) | Ground manipulation, rocks, physical labor |

**Key rule:** Elements don't mix. Cross-element relationships are forbidden. The system exists "for safety" but really for control.

### Training Pairings (Natural Opposites)

Each class trains against its natural enemy - the element that can challenge it. The training grounds are built into the geography of the camp, and each one reflects its elements. The hierarchy is literally built into the architecture.

| Pairing | Name | Location | Dynamic |
|--------|------|----------|---------|
| Wind vs. Fire | **The Scorch Ring** | Inside a volcanic caldera - Fire's home. Thick hot air, hard to breathe, Lyra sweats and gasps. Wind fights at a disadvantage here. | Fire is the only element that can push back against Wind. Adversarial, competitive, fast. Zara trains here. |
| Fire vs. Water | **The Drown Pit** | Sunken stone arena with waterfalls and standing water - Water's home. Fire fights wet, everything is soaked. | Water extinguishes Fire. Natural enemies. Fire fights at a disadvantage here. **This is where Zara met Caspian.** Sparring partners who became something more complicated. |
| Water vs. Earth | **The Break Yard** | Ground level, mud and rock, down with the Earth-shapers - Earth's home. Water comes down here to train. | Water erodes Earth - but Earth has the advantage on its own ground. Water fights at a disadvantage here. **This is where Caspian met Ronan.** Caspian is the only Tide-caller who has been down in the mud. |
| Earth vs. ??? | **None** | They don't train. They labor. No one comes to train against them. | Earth doesn't get sparring partners. They don't get to hone their power. They just work. That isolation is part of why Ronan doesn't speak. |

**What the locations mean:**
- **The Scorch Ring is INSIDE A VOLCANO.** Fire's home turf. Wind fights at a disadvantage - the air is thick, hot, hard to breathe. Lyra should be sweating, gasping, struggling against the heat. That's the point: the hierarchy builds the disadvantage INTO the training grounds. The higher tier always comes down to fight on the lower tier's turf.
- **Zara lives on the side of the volcano.** Not in the Wind tower - she's Flame-kissed, 2nd tier. Her quarters are carved into the volcanic rock, warm and ember-lit. Second highest level in the camp.
- **The Drown Pit is MID-LEVEL, sunken in water.** Water's home. Fire fights wet. Zara learned to fight disadvantaged here - and that's where she met Caspian.
- **The Break Yard is GROUND LEVEL, in the mud.** Earth's home. Water comes down to the bottom of the camp to train here. Caspian has been in the mud. He's walked past Earth-shaper quarters. He's seen how they live. That perspective is WHY he's different from every other Tide-caller.
- **Earth has nothing.** No ring, no pit, no yard. Just labor. They don't even get the dignity of opposition.

**The history in the pairings:**
- Zara and Caspian - met in the Drown Pit. Fire vs. Water. Sparring became something else. The look they exchange in Ep 6 is two people who used to burn each other and maybe still want to.
- Caspian and Ronan - met in the Break Yard. Water vs. Earth. Caspian is the only Tide-caller who ever looked at Ronan like he was more than a laborer. That's because Caspian has been down here. He's seen the mud, the exhaustion, the way Earth-shapers are treated. He's been in it - not by choice, but because that's where Water trains against Earth.
- Ronan's silence - Earth doesn't train. They just work. Who would he talk to? He has Caspian - one person from above who sees him. That's it.

   - Save to Library + download

### Phase 2: Video Generation (Per Episode)
1. **Open Video tab** → select model:
   - Character close-ups & emotional scenes → **Seedance 2.0 R2V** (attach character ref images)
   - Cinematic wide shots + sound effects → **Veo 3.1** (auto-generates audio)
   - Action/chaos scenes → **Sora 2** or **Kling O3**

2. **Use R2V prompt format** for character scenes:
   ```
   [Character description] (image 1) [action] [setting/backdrop] (image 2), [camera angle], [lighting/mood], [sound cue], 9:16
   ```
   Example:
   ```
   A wind-weaver girl with silver-blonde hair and pale luminous skin (image 1) stands awkwardly at a crowded college party (image 2), medium shot, warm golden lighting, uneasy atmosphere, muffled bass music and party chatter, 9:16 vertical
   ```

3. **Queue clips in parallel** - set up next prompt while current one renders

4. **Use "Copy Last Frame and Continue"** to extend scenes seamlessly

### Phase 3: Audio (Audio Tab)
1. **Generate background music** - describe mood with timing cues:
   ```
   First 15 seconds = low bass pulse building tension. At 22 seconds = sudden wind crescendo. At 32 seconds = silence. Then soft heartbeat returns. 75 seconds total, dark romantic fantasy, 9:16
   ```
2. **Generate sound effects individually** - wind, heartbeat, glass breaking, footsteps
3. **Voiceover options:**
   - **Option A: TTS** - Use Venice's TTS models (Orpheus, ElevenLabs Turbo, MiniMax Speech)
   - **Option B: Your voice** - Record on phone, upload audio file to Movie Editor
   - **Recommendation:** Start with your voice for authenticity. Switch to TTS only if needed.

### Phase 4: Edit (Movie Editor Tab)
1. **Drop video clips on timeline** → drag to arrange
2. **Trim clips** by pulling edges
3. **Add voiceover track** on audio layer 2
4. **Add music track** on audio layer 1 (MUSIC IS ALWAYS ADDED HERE - NEVER IN VIDEO PROMPTS)
5. **Drop sound effects** at exact moments on audio layer 3
6. **Use L-cut technique:** Detach audio from clip, extend sound from next scene to start before visual cut (hides AI clip seams)
7. **Add text overlays** (title cards, episode quotes) via title card tool
8. **Apply transitions** (crossfade between clips)
9. **Volume control** per track (duck music during voiceover)

⚠️ **Music rule:** Video clips have NO MUSIC baked in (every prompt says "NO MUSIC, ONLY SOUND EFFECTS"). All music is layered in the Movie Editor. This gives you full control over timing, mood shifts, and volume ducking during voiceover.

### Phase 5: Export & Post
1. **Export** → 9:16, MP4
2. **Post to TikTok** (primary)
3. **Same video → Instagram Reels** (no re-editing needed)
4. **Same video → YouTube Shorts** (optional)

## R2V Prompt Format Reference

Seedance 2.0's Reference-to-Video uses numbered image tags. Here's the exact format:

```
[Action description] (image 1) [preposition] (image 2), [camera], [lighting], [sound effects], NO MUSIC, ONLY SOUND EFFECTS, 9:16 vertical
```

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: No Music in Video Prompts

**Every video prompt must end with `NO MUSIC, ONLY SOUND EFFECTS`.**

Music will be added in post-production (Movie Editor audio layer). Video generation with music baked in sounds wrong and can't be fixed. Sound effects in the prompt help Seedance understand the mood and timing.

### Multi-Scene Prompts (Seedance 2.0 Feature)

Seedance 2.0 can generate **up to 3 scene cuts in a single video**. This is a HUGE credit saver - instead of generating 3 separate clips (3×15-25 credits), you describe the sequence in one prompt (1×15-25 credits).

**Format for multi-scene:**
```
[Scene 1 description] - CUT TO - [Scene 2 description] - CUT TO - [Scene 3 description], (image 1) [preposition] (image 2), [camera], [sound effects], NO MUSIC, ONLY SOUND EFFECTS, 9:16 vertical
```

**Example - 3 scenes in one prompt:**
```
A wind-weaver girl with silver-blonde hair and pale luminous skin (image 1) stands awkwardly at a crowded party, looking uncomfortable - CUT TO - extreme close-up of her wide panicked eyes, heartbeat rising - CUT TO - a tall earth-shaper man (image 3) appears in the doorway, backlit, imposing, cinematic fantasy illustration - painterly, jewel-tone palette, volumetric light, muffled party bass, heartbeat rising, door creak, NO MUSIC, ONLY SOUND EFFECTS, 9:16 vertical
```

**When to use multi-scene:**
- ✅ Sequential moments that flow naturally (setup → tension → reveal)
- ✅ Same character across continuous action (walking → stopping → turning)
- ✅ Montage moments (training sequence, passage of time)
- ❌ Scenes requiring very different camera angles (close-up → wide → close-up - hard for AI to parse)
- ❌ Scenes with complex sound design per cut (keep those separate)
- ❌ Key emotional beats that need perfect timing (keep those separate for editing control)

**12-second clips are the sweet spot.** 5-6 seconds rushes past moments that deserve to land. 12 seconds gives each beat breathing room, lets the audience feel the emotion, and matches TikTok's viewing rhythm. Multi-scene clips use the full 12s - each CUT TO gets ~4 seconds, which is the minimum for a cut to register.

**Prepositions that work well:**
- walks through, runs across, stands in, looks at, reaches toward
- emerges from, enters, approaches, turns away from

**Example prompts from Venice's own guide:**
- "A lone scavenger (image 1) trudges through a blizzard on a frozen planet (image 2), wide cinematic shot, camera tracks backwards slowly, wind howling, NO MUSIC, ONLY SOUND EFFECTS, 9:16 vertical"
- "Image 1 walks through image 2, NO MUSIC, ONLY SOUND EFFECTS, 9:16 vertical"  ← simplest format, works great

**For non-character scenes (no R2V needed):**
- Just describe the scene directly: "Dynamic wide shot, wind explosion erupting from a young woman at a party, drinks flying, NO MUSIC, ONLY SOUND EFFECTS, 9:16 vertical"
