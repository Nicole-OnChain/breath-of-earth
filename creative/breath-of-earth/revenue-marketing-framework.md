## Posting Strategy — TikTok (Primary Platform)

### Best Times to Post (US Audience)

| Day | Best Windows (ET) | Reason |
|-----|-------------------|--------|
| Mon-Fri | 7-9 AM, 12-2 PM, 7-10 PM | Commute + lunch + evening scroll |
| Saturday | 9-11 AM, 7-9 PM | Lazy morning + evening wind-down |
| Sunday | 10 AM-12 PM, 8-10 PM | Brunch scroll + before-bed |

### Posting Frequency

| Phase | Frequency | Content Mix |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Launch (Weeks 1-4) | 1-2x/day | Episodes + behind-the-scenes + teasers |
| Growth (Months 2-3) | 1x/day | Episodes on schedule + 1-2 engagement posts |
| Steady (Month 4+) | 4-5x/week | Episodes 3x/week + engagement 1-2x |

### Episode Release Strategy

**Option A: Serialized (Recommended for launch)**
- Post 1 episode/day for first 11 days (blitz to hook audience)
- Then slow to 3x/week for Season 2
- Advantage: binge momentum, TikTok algorithm rewards daily posting
- Risk: burn through content fast

**Option B: Scheduled**
- Post 3 episodes/week (Mon/Wed/Fri)
- Advantage: sustained presence over 4 weeks
- Risk: slower momentum, harder to build binge audience

**My recommendation: Option A for launch.** Blitz Season 1 over 11 days. Get people hooked. Then maintain with behind-the-scenes, character art, and engagement content while writing Season 2. TikTok rewards daily posting in the first 30 days — that's when the algorithm decides if you're worth pushing.

### Content Mix (Non-Episode Posts)

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |

| Behind-the-scenes | 2-3x/week | "How I made this" — Venice Studio walkthroughs |

## Posting Strategy — YouTube Shorts

### Why YouTube Shorts (Not Long-Form Yet)

- Lower barrier to entry than full YouTube videos
- Same 9:16 format as TikTok/IG Reels
- Ad revenue is lower but it compounds over time
- Acts as discovery layer — drives traffic to TikTok/IG for the main audience
- Long-term: compile Season 1 into a single 15-20 min video on main YouTube

### YouTube-Specific Adjustments

- Add end screen pointing to next episode
- Longer description with full story context
- Pin comment with episode playlist link
- Post 24-48 hours AFTER TikTok (YouTube algorithm is slower)

### BookTok/Novella Channel Discovery Playbook

Hashtags alone don't get you into BookTok. The algorithm needs to *see* you belong there. Here's how:

1. **Use trending BookTok sounds.** Search "booktok sound" or "romancetok sound" on TikTok — audio is 50% of how the algorithm categorizes you. Use the same dramatic/emo tracks other novella creators use.

2. **Post in the first 30 minutes of peak hours.** BookTok lives 7-10 PM ET. Post then.

3. **Comment on other BookTok/novella videos.** Not "check out my series!" — genuine, thoughtful comments on their content. Your username appears, curious people click through.

4. **Stitch/Duet trending BookTok videos.** React to "if you liked X, you'll love Y" prompts with your clips. This is the #1 way novella channels blow up.

5. **The "POV" format.** Your ep1 hook is perfect for this:
   > POV: you're the earth girl who doesn't belong anywhere, and the air commander just appeared 🔥

BookTok's algorithm categorizes by **sound + hashtag + watch time**. The novella/series crowd specifically uses `#storytime`, `#slowburn`, `#fantasytiktok`, and `#booktok` together. That combo is your signal flare.
