## Import Instructions

### Method 1: Import as Markdown (Recommended)

1. Open Novel Crafter → New Project → "Breath of Earth"
2. Go to **Notes** section
3. Drag the entire `world/` folder into Notes — each file becomes a separate note
4. Drag the entire `characters/` folder into Notes — each file becomes a character sheet
5. Go to **Manuscript** section
6. Create a new chapter titled "Chapter 1: Awakening"
7. Copy the content from `chapters/ch01-awakening.md` into the chapter
8. The beat structure, prose notes, and word count targets will be preserved

### Method 2: Use Novel Crafter's Codex

1. Open Novel Crafter → New Project → "Breath of Earth"
2. In the **Codex** panel, create entries for each character:
   - Lyra (Wind-weaver, POV)
   - Ronan (Earth-shaper)
   - Zara (Flame-kissed)
   - Caspian (Tide-caller)
   - Sera (No element)
   - The Twins (Fire, unmanifested)
   - Mom and Dad (Memory)
3. Copy the relevant details from each `characters/` file into the Codex entries
4. In the Codex, create World entries:
   - The Elemental Hierarchy
   - The Camp (locations)
   - The Elements (powers)
   - Elemental Resonance
   - The Enforcers
5. Create the Timeline using the `world/timeline.md` file

### Method 3: Quick Start (Fastest)

1. Open Novel Crafter → New Project → "Breath of Earth"
2. Import all `.md` files at once using Novel Crafter's bulk import
3. Move character files to Character Codex
4. Move world files to World Codex
5. Move chapter files to Manuscript

## What's Ready vs. What You Write

### ✅ Ready (I built this)
- Full world bible with hierarchy, camp locations, element rules
- Character profiles with voice, arc, fears, desires, writing notes
- Chapter 1 beat sheet with scene-by-scene breakdown
- Master timeline covering all 11 chapters
- Cross-reference table for story threads
- Prose notes for Chapter 1 (voice, tone, what to avoid)

### ✍️ You Write (This is yours)
- The actual prose. The sentences that make someone feel something.
- Lyra's voice in practice — the rhythm, the pauses, the words that land
- The moments between beats — what she's thinking, what she notices, what she doesn't say
- The sensory details that make the camp feel real
- The silence that means more than dialogue

### 🔄 We Collaborate (This is us)
- I review every chapter you write for consistency, pacing, timeline
- I flag character voice drift, world rule violations, or plot holes
- I draft scenes you're stuck on — you rewrite in your voice
- I track every detail across chapters so nothing slips

## Writing Workflow

1. **Open Novel Crafter** → Go to Chapter 1
2. **Read the beat sheet** in the chapter notes
3. **Write the scene** following the beats and prose notes
4. **Don't overthink** — short sentences, Lyra's voice, what happened not how it felt
5. **When you finish a chapter**, send it to me
6. **I review** for: consistency, timeline, character voice, pacing, plot holes
7. **You revise** based on feedback
8. **Next chapter** — I'll have the beat sheet ready before you start

## Voice Reference

Lyra's voice is the whole book. Get this right and everything else follows.

**Short. Punchy. Honest.**

❌ "I found myself reluctantly attending a gathering that felt entirely alien to my sensibilities."

✅ "I didn't want to be there."

❌ "The mysterious stranger emanated an aura of quiet strength that drew me inexorably forward."

✅ "He didn't look back. Didn't need to."

❌ "My heart raced as his hand made contact with my back, sending electric sensations cascading through my body."

✅ "Just his hand on my back."

The poetry is in what happens. Not in how you describe it.
