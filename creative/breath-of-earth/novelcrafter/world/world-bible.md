## The Elemental Hierarchy

The world is organized by element. Where you sit in the hierarchy determines how you live, who you train with, who you're allowed to love.

| Tier | Element | Title | Position | Quarters |
|------|---------|-------|----------|----------|
| 1st | Wind | Weaver | Top | The Wind Tower — highest point in camp |
| 2nd | Fire | Flame-kissed | Second | Volcano-side quarters — warm, ember-lit |
| 3rd | Water | Tide-caller | Third | Mid-level, near water | 
| 4th | Earth | Shaper | Bottom | Ground level — mud, stone, labor |

### The Rules
1. **Elements don't mix.** Cross-element relationships are forbidden. The system says it's "for safety." It's really for control.
2. **Training is hierarchical.** Higher tiers come down to train on lower-tier turf. The advantage is always built into the architecture.
3. **Earth doesn't train.** They labor. No one comes to spar against them. They have no ring, no pit, no yard. They have work.
4. **The hierarchy is self-reinforcing.** Wind trains against Fire on Fire's turf and still wins. Fire trains against Water on Water's turf and often loses. The system proves itself right by stacking the odds.

## The Camp

The camp is built into the side of a volcanic island. The hierarchy is literally built into the architecture.

### The Wind Tower (Top)
- Highest point in camp
- Open-air balconies, constant breeze
- Lyra's quarters after Zara recruits her
- Where the wind-weavers sleep and train
- Zara gives Lyra a tour from here — "Better air flow. Obviously."

### Volcano-Side Quarters (Second Level)
- Carved into volcanic rock
- Warm, ember-lit, comfortable
- Zara lives here
- The Scorch Ring is inside the volcano caldera — Fire's home turf

### The Scorch Ring
- Inside the volcanic caldera
- Fire trains here. Wind comes down to train here.
- Thick hot air. Wind-weavers gasp and sweat. The disadvantage is structural.
- Zara knocks Lyra flat here in Ch 7.

### The Drown Pit (Mid-Level)
- Sunken stone arena with waterfalls and standing water
- Water's home turf. Fire comes here to train.
- Where Zara and Caspian met. Fire vs. Water. Sparring became something else.
- Where Caspian watches the fight in Ch 9.

### The Break Yard (Ground Level)
- Mud and rock. Bottom of the camp.
- Earth's home — but they don't train here. They work here.
- Ronan carries stones here. It's not training. It's labor.
- Water comes down here to train against Earth. That's how Caspian met Ronan.

### The Cell (Below Ground)
- Iron bars. Punishment for Earth-shapers who break rules.
- Where Ronan is held after he raises the rock wall in Ch 9.
- Where Lyra visits him in Ch 10.
- Where his hand shifts toward hers through the bars.

## The Elements

### Wind (Weaver)
- Air manipulation, storms, wind control
- Highest tier — fastest, most versatile
- Lyra's element. Responds to emotion. When she's scared, it erupts. When she's resolved, it sharpens.
- Training: against Fire, on Fire's turf (Scorch Ring)

### Fire (Flame-kissed)
- Fire creation, heat, passion-fueled
- Second tier. Powerful but less versatile than Wind.
- Zara's element. Confident, warm, aggressive.
- Training: against Water (Drown Pit) and against Wind (Scorch Ring)

### Water (Tide-caller)
- Water control, mist, tidal forces
- Third tier. Perceptive, adaptive.
- Caspian's element. He uses it thoughtfully, not aggressively.
- Training: against Earth (Break Yard) and against Fire (Drown Pit)

### Earth (Shaper)
- Ground manipulation, rocks, physical strength
- Lowest tier. Powerful but treated as labor, not warriors.
- Ronan's element. He doesn't speak. The earth moves with him.
- No training partner. No ring. Just work.

## Elemental Resonance

What Lyra and Ronan feel when they touch — the sparks, the warmth, the pull — is elemental resonance. Wind and Earth don't train against each other. The hierarchy keeps them separated entirely.

But opposites don't just clash. They complete.

The connection is not attraction. It's not chemistry. It's something older than the system that tries to contain it. The ground shifts for him. The air moves for her. When they touch, the elements recognize each other.

The hierarchy exists to prevent exactly this.

## The Enforcers

- System muscle. No element shown.
- They come for Lyra in Ch 9. Ronan steps between.
- They take Ronan to the Cell. They enforce hierarchy rules.
- Not individuals. Not characters. They are the system made physical.
