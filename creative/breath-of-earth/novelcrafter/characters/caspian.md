# Caspian — Character Profile

**Novel:** Breath of Earth — Book 1
**Role:** The truth-teller. The one who sees from both sides.
**Element:** Tide-caller (3rd tier, Water)
**Age:** Early-mid 20s
**First appearance:** Chapter 5 (The River) — first look. Full interaction in Chapter 6.

---

## Physical Description
- Striking dark-skinned man with compelling light-colored eyes (pale blue-green)
- Blue-teal clothing with water motifs
- Water mist drifting around his shoulders when he's near water
- Calculating expression. Not cold — perceptive. He sees everything.
- Broad shoulders. Built like someone who trained against stronger elements.

## Voice
- Blunt. Direct. Doesn't waste words.
- "Watch your step." (Ch 5) — it's about the river AND about the camp.
- Questions that cut to the truth: "Wind-weaver?" Not impressed. Just measuring.
- He doesn't soften things. But he doesn't lie either.

## Why He's Different
He's the only Tide-caller who's been down in the mud. He's trained in the Break Yard against Earth-shapers. He's seen how they live. He's walked past their quarters. He's been in it — not by choice, but because that's where Water trains against Earth.

That perspective is why he sees Ronan. Not just looks at him. Sees him.

## Core Fear
- That his connection to Zara means something that the system can't contain
- That he's been training against Earth-shapers and never questioned why they don't get to train back
- That seeing clearly doesn't mean he can change anything

## Core Desire
- To understand. To see the system for what it is.
- To not be blind, even when seeing is uncomfortable.

## History with Zara
They trained in the Drown Pit. Fire vs. Water. Enemies on the mat, something else off it. The history is unresolved. The electricity between them is real. But so is the hostility.

He's the one who walked away. She's the one who didn't forgive him for it.

## Writing Caspian
- He should feel like the only honest person in any room.
- His bluntness is not cruelty. It's respect. He thinks you can handle the truth.
- When he helps Lyra find Ronan (Ch 10), it's not out of friendship. It's because someone should, and no one else will.
- His relationship with Zara is unfinished business. Never resolve it in Book 1. Just let it crackle.
