# Lyra — Character Profile

**Novel:** Breath of Earth — Book 1
**Role:** POV character, protagonist
**Element:** Wind-weaver (1st tier)
**Age:** Early 20s
**First appearance:** Chapter 1 (Awakening)

---

## Physical Description
- Silver-blonde hair, windswept and unruly
- Fair skin, wide expressive eyes (blue-gray)
- Lean, athletic build — not delicate, not imposing
- Wears simple, flowing fabrics before the camp; white/silver camp clothes after

## Voice
- First person, present tense
- Short sentences. Punchy. No decoration.
- Wry humor that surfaces when she's nervous
- Honest to a fault — she doesn't lie to herself
- Example: "I didn't want to be there." Not "I found myself reluctantly attending." Just the flat truth.

## Core Fear
- That she got pulled into this world and her whole family got dragged in with her
- That belonging to the hierarchy means losing the people she loves
- That she'll become what the system wants her to be

## Core Desire
- To protect her family (Sera, the twins, Mom and Dad)
- To understand what she is and what the connection with Ronan means
- To not lose herself in the process

## What She Doesn't Know Yet
- Why the wind responds to her emotions
- That her connection to Ronan is elemental (Wind and Earth are opposites in the training system)
- That Sera has no element (and what that will mean)
- That the hierarchy is not just unfair — it's designed to prevent exactly what she's feeling

## Emotional Arc (Book 1)
1. **Displacement** (Ch 1-2): Doesn't belong. Doesn't understand.
2. **Connection** (Ch 3-4): The promise. The night. Something is happening between them.
3. **Belonging** (Ch 5-6): The camp. A place. A role. But she sees what it costs.
4. **Conflict** (Ch 7-8): The hierarchy in action. The system closing in.
5. **Choice** (Ch 9-11): She chooses him over the system. Not because it's romantic. Because it's right.

## Relationships
- **Ronan:** The connection. Not love yet — something older and stranger. Recognition before understanding.
- **Sera:** Twin sister. The shadow. They look alike and that's where it ends.
- **Zara:** Recruiter. Warm cage. "Sweetheart" and "princess" that feel like both kindness and control.
- **Caspian:** The one who sees clearly. Blunt, perceptive. Not a friend, but someone who tells the truth.
- **The twins:** Two red-headed little brothers. She's fighting to get back to them.
- **Mom and Dad:** Memory. Home. The thing she lost.

## What Makes Her Different From Other Protagonists
- She's not chosen one. She's not special because of destiny. She's someone whose power erupted at the worst possible moment, and now she has to deal with it.
- She doesn't want to be here. That's important. She's not the hero who rises to the occasion. She's the person who got dragged into something and decides, piece by piece, what she's willing to fight for.
- Her rebellion is instinctive, not ideological. She doesn't have a theory about why the hierarchy is wrong. She just feels it.

## Dialogue Style
- Short. Often just one sentence.
- Questions she doesn't wait for answers to.
- Statements that land like observations.
- "I didn't want to be there." / "Keep up." / "Why was I always looking down?"
