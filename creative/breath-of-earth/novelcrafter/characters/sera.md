# Sera — Character Profile

**Novel:** Breath of Earth — Book 1
**Role:** The shadow. The seed.
**Element:** None (yet — learns to steal energy in later seasons)
**Age:** Early 20s (Lyra's twin)
**First appearance:** Chapter 3 (The Sister) — flashback

---

## Physical Description
- Almost identical to Lyra — but not quite
- Brown-blonde hair (not silver-blonde)
- Darker, harder eyes. Where Lyra looks lost, Sera looks calculating.
- Same face, different expression. The difference is in how they hold it.

## Who She Is in Book 1
Sera is a seed. A shadow. She appears once, in flashback (Ch 3), and the reader should feel something is off without being able to name it.

She's Lyra's twin sister. They look alike. That's where it ends.

Sera has no element. In a world defined by elements, she's nothing. Her mom and dad loved all four kids the same. But the world doesn't. Lyra has Wind. The boys will have Fire. Everyone in this family has something coming.

Everyone except Sera.

## What She Becomes (Later Seasons)
She learns to steal energy from people and the land. Bad magic. Taught by a mysterious man. She becomes the villain.

But in Book 1, she's just a seed. A cold look. A twin who isn't grateful. A shadow in the corner of a warm memory.

## Writing Sera in Book 1
- Don't over-explain her. One scene. One look. The reader should feel the chill.
- She's not overtly hostile. She's just... not warm. In a memory full of warmth, she's the draft.
- The reader should wonder about her. Not hate her. Not yet.
- Her isolation should feel real, not villainous. Everyone in her family has something. She has nothing. That's a wound, not a motive — yet.
