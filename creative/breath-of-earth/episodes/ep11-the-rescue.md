## WHAT THIS EPISODE DOES

Lyra comes back. She uses her wind to create a distraction — not a weapon, a shield. While the enforcers respond to the windstorm, she gets Ronan out. He speaks for the first time in the entire series: "You shouldn't be here." Five words.

They escape through the jungle. And for the first time, we see Ronan's full power. The earth clears their path. Not because he's angry. Not because he's forced. Because it knows him. He's been suppressing this his whole life. Now he's not.

The episode ends with them running through the jungle, free. But Lyra looks back. Her family is still inside. She made a choice. It has a cost.

Season 1 ends on the run. They're out. But they're not safe.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES

### Lyra
She came back. Ep 8 — her wind exploded. Ep 9 — he saved her. Ep 10 — she found him. Ep 11 — she's getting him out. She uses her wind as a distraction, not a weapon. She's learning control. She's learning choice. The windstorm is precise. Surgical. She's not the girl who lost control in the Scorch Ring anymore.

### Ronan
He speaks. Five words. "You shouldn't be here." That's it. The audience has been waiting 11 episodes to hear his voice. Don't give him more. Five words is enough. And then we see his power — the earth clearing their path through the jungle. This is who he is when he's not at the bottom. This is who he's been suppressing.

### The Escape
She doesn't break the bars. She can't bend stone. She uses the windstorm to draw the enforcers away. While they're responding to the "natural" disturbance, she gets him out. His hand is already waiting. He knew she'd come back.
