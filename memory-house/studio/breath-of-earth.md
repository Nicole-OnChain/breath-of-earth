## Key Production Rules
- **Duration:** 6 clips × 12s = 72+ seconds per episode
- **Resolution:** 720p generation, 1080p export
- **Style:** Cinematic fantasy illustration — painterly, jewel-tone palette, volumetric light
- **Music:** NEVER in video prompts — always added in post via Movie Editor
- **Character identifiers (filter-safe):**
  - Lyra: "wind-weaver girl with silver-blonde hair and pale luminous skin"
  - Ronan: "earth-shaper man with dark hair and stone-grey eyes"
  - Zara: "flame-kissed woman with vivid red hair and ember-glow eyes"
  - Caspian: "tide-caller man with dark skin and bioluminescent teal eyes"
- **Threatening figures:** Use "dark-haired figure" + architectural descriptions ("arm braced on wall beside her head, caging her in")
- **Ep 1 pre-Compound outfit:** Plain dark zip jacket over white crew-neck tee, dark denim jeans, no jewelry

## Characters
- **Lyra** (Wind-weaver, POV) — silver-blonde hair, pale luminous skin. Born of mixed-element parents. Twin. The spark with Ronan is recognition.
- **Ronan** (Earth-shaper, protector) — dark hair, stone-grey eyes. Cold exterior, hidden warmth.
- **Zara** (Flame-kissed) — vivid red hair, ember-glow eyes. S1 frenemy → S2 love story → S3 kills Caspian (forced), becomes double agent, destroys hierarchy from inside
- **Caspian** (Tide-caller) — dark skin, bioluminescent teal eyes. History with Zara. S1-S2 love interest. **Dies early S3** — possibly by his own plan, sending Zara into the hierarchy. His death is the catalyst.
- **Caspian's uncle** (Water, S3+) — has Caspian's traits. Hierarchy loyalist who explains why the system exists. Walks the line with Zara all season. Not a love interest — intellectual partnership built on shared loss.

## Character Arcs
- **Zara:** Frenemy → lover → broken (Caspian dies) → walks the line with the uncle → appears to flip → double agent → vindicated
- **Lyra:** Awakening → discovering heritage (twin, mixed parents) → uniting the four → overthrow
- **Ronan:** Cold protector → vulnerable → fighting the system that kept him down
- **Caspian:** Loving, loyal → dies early S3, possibly by his own plan → his death sends Zara into the hierarchy
- **Uncle:** Hierarchy loyalist → explains why the system exists (makes sense!) → challenged by Zara → eventual turn

## World-Building
- Power comes from specific locations on Earth (source points) — see `studio/breath-of-earth-world-bible.md`
- Four elements united are unstoppable — that's why the hierarchy keeps them apart
- The hierarchy is geography weaponized into caste, not natural order
- Season arc: S1 Awakening → S2 The Sister → S3 Overthrow → S4+ The Sources
