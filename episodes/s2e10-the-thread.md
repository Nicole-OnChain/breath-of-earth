## WHAT THIS EPISODE DOES

Lyra starts receiving information from Zara through small signs — a guard rotation schedule left where only Lyra would find it, a whispered warning about an inspection. She starts to understand: Zara didn't switch sides. She's inside. But the cost is brutal — Zara has to participate in things that make Lyra sick to watch. The hierarchy tightens. Lyra's combat training becomes mandatory — they're weaponizing her. She has to learn to fight for real. Not for show. For survival.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES

### Lyra
She's being trained to kill. Not to defend — to attack. The hierarchy doesn't want her controlled. They want her pointed. Her wind blasts are getting stronger. She doesn't like what she's becoming.

### Zara
Every piece of information she passes to Lyra costs her. She has to stand beside the uncle and watch the camp suffer. She has to participate in inspections. She has to nod when they tighten restrictions. And she has to do it without breaking. The audience is starting to see — she's not a traitor. She's a spy.
