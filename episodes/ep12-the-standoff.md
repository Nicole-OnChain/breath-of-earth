## WHAT THIS EPISODE DOES

They run into the Break Yard. For a moment it feels like they might make it. Then the guards descend from every level. Torches. Weapons. Every exit blocked.

Lyra's wind picks up. Ronan's earth shifts. They're ready to fight. But there are too many.

Then — water. From above. Caspian on the upper level, one hand raised, a wall of water crashing down into the Break Yard. It sweeps guards off their feet, washes away everything — but parts around Ronan and Lyra like they're standing in the eye of a storm.

The water clears a path. They run. They're out. And we didn't look back.

This is Caspian's moment. The only Tide-caller who would think to do this. The one who's been down in the mud. He saves them. And they don't even know it was him — not yet.

## CHARACTER NOTES

### Lyra
She just broke Ronan out. She just held his hand. She's running on adrenaline and wind. When the guards surround them, her instinct is to fight. Then the water comes — not at them, for them — and she doesn't question it. She runs.

### Ronan
He just stepped through broken bars. His earth is still moving. When the guards surround them, he's ready — not angry, not desperate, just steady. The ground shifts under his feet. He'll fight if he has to. Then the water comes, and he recognizes something in it. Something controlled. Something that knows exactly where they're standing.

### Caspian
He's been watching. He's the only Tide-caller who would do this — not because he's brave, but because he's been down in the mud with them. He knows what it's like to be at the bottom. One hand raised. A wall of water. And then he's gone. They don't see his face. Not yet.
