## WHAT THIS EPISODE DOES

Lyra enters the camp for the first time. She's alone — Ronan left. Caspian is the first person who actually talks to her. Then Zara arrives and claims her — pulling her away from Caspian with a look that says *we have history*. Zara brings Lyra to the Wind-weavers at the top. Lyra looks down and sees Ronan carrying stones at the bottom. The hierarchy: Air → Fire → Water → Earth. Ronan is Earth. The man who held her is at the bottom.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES

### Caspian (Tide-caller)
First real interaction. He's blunt and perceptive — doesn't flirt, doesn't charm, just sees her. Where Ronan is a wall, Caspian is a mirror. But Zara cuts in — and the look between them says they have history. Caspian's jaw tightening isn't about Lyra. It's about Zara.

### Zara (Flame-kissed)
First appearance. She doesn't just welcome Lyra — she claims her. Pulls her away from Caspian specifically. Not because she's mean. Because a Wind-weaver with a Tide-caller is below them both. Zara's hand on Lyra's shoulder is possessive. Her "not down here with them" is aimed at Caspian. The warmth is real. The calculation is also real. She and Caspian have history — the look they exchange says it all.
